3. Accuracy Disclaimer

Why Does Mentora Make Mistakes? 🤔
AI Lacks Human-Like Understanding​
Mentora is built on advanced natural language processing models like GPT-4, which have
been trained on vast amounts of text data. However, it does not "think" or "understand"
like a human—it predicts words and phrases based on patterns rather than genuine
comprehension. This means:

●​ It may misinterpret complex, vague, or ambiguous questions.
●​ It does not have real-world experience like a human expert.
●​ It cannot independently verify the latest news or updates unless explicitly

programmed to do so.

Limited Scope of Knowledge
Mentora is specifically designed to answer questions related to the app’s content level
and study path. It cannot provide information beyond this scope. If you ask questions
unrelated to the app's subject matter or its practice question, responses may be irrelevant,
incomplete, or incorrect.
Mentora does not replace external resources, such as official textbooks, real-world expert
advice, or professional consultations.
If a topic is outside its training data, it may try to generate a response based on similar
concepts, which can lead to inaccuracies.

Variability in User Input
People communicate in different ways, and while Mentora is trained to understand a wide
range of expressions, it may struggle with:

●​ Unusual phrasing or slang that doesn’t match typical study-related questions.

●​ Highly technical or domain-specific queries that require expertise beyond its
knowledge base.

●​ Incomplete or unclear questions, where it must guess the user’s intent.

Data Limitations and Possible Biases
Mentora learns from publicly available sources and curated training materials. However,
this means:

●​ Some responses might be based on outdated, incomplete, or biased information.
●​ AI can reflect biases present in the data it was trained on.
●​ It does not have real-time access to confidential, proprietary, or unpublished

sources.
